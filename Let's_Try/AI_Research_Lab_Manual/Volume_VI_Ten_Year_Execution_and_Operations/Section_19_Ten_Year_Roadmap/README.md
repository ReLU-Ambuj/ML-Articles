**Volume:** VI — Ten-Year Execution & Operations  
**Status:** 🔲 Not Started

## Objectives

1. Build a concrete, milestone-based 10-year roadmap with quarterly granularity for Years 1–3
2. Define success metrics for each year across: research, hiring, funding, infrastructure, products, and revenue
3. Understand what triggers acceleration vs. what signals a pivot

---

## Roadmap Overview

```
YEAR 1: Solo Researcher Lab
   └── Goal: First publication, open source presence, compute access

YEAR 2: First Hires, First Funding
   └── Goal: Team of 3–5, seed funding or grants, 3–5 papers

YEAR 3: Research Identity
   └── Goal: Known for 1 research area, 10+ papers, first revenue

YEAR 4: Scaling Research
   └── Goal: 10+ team, own compute cluster, Series A or major grant

YEAR 5: Lab Credibility
   └── Goal: NeurIPS oral / spotlight, product launch, $2M+ ARR or grant budget

YEAR 6–7: Competitive Position
   └── Goal: Cited by frontier labs, talent coming to you, 25–50 people

YEAR 8–9: Organizational Maturity
   └── Goal: Multiple research directions, sustainable funding, 50–100 people

YEAR 10: Global Research Organization
   └── Goal: Competing at frontier, respected globally, sustainable mission
```

## Prompt for Deep Content Generation

```
You are a research lab CEO, strategic planner, and venture capitalist 
with experience building research organizations over 10+ years.

Write Section 19 of "Building an AI Research Lab From Scratch" — 
a detailed 10-year roadmap for a solo researcher building an AI lab 
starting with minimal capital in India in 2026.

This must be specific and realistic — not aspirational. Include failure scenarios.

For EACH year (Year 1 through Year 10), provide:

   MILESTONES:
   - Research milestones (papers, models, benchmarks, citations)
   - Hiring milestones (team size, key roles added)
   - Funding milestones (amounts raised, sources)
   - Infrastructure milestones (compute capacity, tools deployed)
   - Product/revenue milestones (first dollar, first enterprise customer, ARR)
   - Community milestones (GitHub stars, Discord members, Twitter followers)

   KEY DECISIONS:
   - The 1–3 most important decisions in this year that determine the next year's trajectory
   - Decision trade-offs: what you gain and what you sacrifice with each choice
   - Decision triggers: what information or events should prompt each decision?

   RISKS:
   - The top 3 risks that could derail this year
   - Mitigation strategies for each risk

   METRICS TO TRACK WEEKLY:
   - Quantitative indicators that the year is on track

   BUDGET:
   - Realistic monthly burn rate for this year
   - Revenue/funding expectation

Now provide a SYNTHESIS section:

   YEAR 1 QUARTERLY BREAKDOWN (Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 granularity):
   - Week-by-week focus for Q1 (the most critical quarter)
   - Monthly milestones for Q2–Q4
   
   CONDITIONAL BRANCHING:
   - "If Year 3 produces no papers at tier-1 venues, here is what you do..."
   - "If Year 2 funding doesn't materialize, here is the fallback..."
   - "If you get a talent offer from Google/OpenAI in Year 2, here is how to think about it..."
   
   COMPARISON TO KNOWN LAB TRAJECTORIES:
   - How does this roadmap compare to OpenAI Year 1–5?
   - How does it compare to Mistral AI (founded 2023, €600M Series B in 12 months)?
   - How does it compare to Sakana AI (founded 2023, Japanese small lab)?
   - What can a solo founder with ₹5L realistically achieve vs. a funded team?

Format: structured markdown, milestone tables per year,
quarterly Gantt-style timeline for Year 1, conditional decision tree.
Depth target: ~25–30 pages.
```

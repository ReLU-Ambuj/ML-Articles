# Section 23 — Daily Founder Schedule

## Objectives

1. Design a daily operating rhythm that maximizes research output per hour
2. Understand how to segment time between research, operations, and recruiting
3. Build sustainable habits for 10–16 hour workdays without burnout

---

## Prompt for Deep Content Generation

```
You are a research lab founder, productivity systems designer, and 
cognitive performance researcher writing for a solo AI lab founder.

Write Section 23 of "Building an AI Research Lab From Scratch" — 
a practical daily operating guide for an AI research lab founder.

No motivation. Only systems, time allocation, and decision protocols.

PART A — TIME BUDGET ANALYSIS
   The solo founder has approximately 14 hours of productive time per day.
   How should these be allocated?

   Analyze the allocation for three phases:
   Phase 1 — Pure Researcher (Year 0–1): no team, all research
   Phase 2 — Research + Lab Building (Year 1–3): team growing, split attention
   Phase 3 — Lab Director (Year 3+): managing research agenda + operations

   For each phase: create a time budget pie chart (in ASCII) and justify each allocation.

PART B — SAMPLE DAILY SCHEDULES
   Design hour-by-hour schedules for:
   
   1. DEEP RESEARCH DAY (2–3× per week)
      - No meetings, no email, pure research
      - Morning: mathematical work and paper reading
      - Afternoon: coding and experiments
      - Evening: writing and idea journaling
   
   2. RESEARCH + OPERATIONS DAY (2× per week)
      - Morning: research
      - Afternoon: recruiting, funding, operations
      - Evening: community engagement (GitHub, Discord, Twitter)
   
   3. READING DAY (1× per week)
      - Full day: reading new papers, exploring new areas
      - No experiments, no coding
   
   4. ADMIN DAY (1× per week)
      - Email, contracts, accounting, legal, hiring admin
      - Keep this to one day/week maximum

PART C — MORNING PROTOCOL
   What the first 2 hours of the day should contain:
   - No email, no social media in the first 90 minutes
   - The "research journal" morning entry: 3 questions to answer before anything else
   - Physical exercise as cognitive primer (evidence-based)
   - The daily research question: what is the ONE thing you will understand better today?

PART D — EXPERIMENT REVIEW PROTOCOL
   Daily experiment review: how to evaluate overnight training runs
   - What metrics to check first (loss, eval metrics, compute utilization)
   - Decision tree: keep running / kill / modify / replicate
   - Logging findings: 3-sentence entry per experiment result
   - When a result surprises you: the investigation protocol

PART E — FUNDRAISING TIME BUDGET
   When to do fundraising without destroying research rhythm:
   - Fundraising sprints: 6-week intensives, not year-round
   - Investor update emails: one per month, 15 minutes to write
   - Conference networking: use paper presentations as networking events
   - Keeping a CRM for investors: simple Notion or Airtable system

PART F — READING SCHEDULE
   How to read 5–10 papers per week:
   - Morning reading vs. evening reading: which types of papers for which time
   - The "paper queue" system: how to manage 100+ papers in backlog
   - When to stop reading and start experimenting

PART G — WRITING SCHEDULE
   Daily writing practice:
   - Research notes: 30 minutes per day minimum
   - Paper writing: block 2–3 hours, 3× per week when in writing mode
   - Blog/Twitter: 30 minutes 2–3× per week
   - The "write before you understand" principle

PART H — ENERGY MANAGEMENT
   - The cognitive energy model: when to do hard thinking vs. mechanical tasks
   - Ultradian rhythm: 90-minute focus blocks with 20-minute breaks
   - Sleep optimization for research productivity: 8 hours is not optional
   - Nutrition and exercise for 10+ hour workdays

Format: structured markdown, hour-by-hour schedule tables,
time budget ASCII charts, weekly calendar template.
Depth target: ~10–15 pages.
```

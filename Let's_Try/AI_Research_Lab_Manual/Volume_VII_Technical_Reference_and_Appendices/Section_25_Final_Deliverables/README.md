## Prompt for Deep Content Generation

```
You are a research lab founder, management consultant, and strategic planner 
creating the foundational documents for a new AI research organization.

Write Section 25 of "Building an AI Research Lab From Scratch" — 
the 10 final deliverable documents that constitute the AI Lab Blueprint.

Each document should be:
- Immediately usable as a real organizational document
- Specific to a solo founder starting an AI research lab in India in 2026
- Designed to be updated quarterly as the lab grows
- Graduate/PhD-level depth in technical sections

DOCUMENT 1 — COMPLETE AI RESEARCH LAB BLUEPRINT
   A master overview document (5–8 pages) covering:
   - Mission statement and research values
   - Lab archetype and intended evolution
   - Research philosophy and publication policy
   - Compute strategy (current and 5-year plan)
   - Team philosophy and culture principles
   - Relationship to the broader AI research community

DOCUMENT 2 — ORGANIZATIONAL HANDBOOK
   A cultural and operational reference (8–10 pages) covering:
   - How decisions get made (explicit decision-making framework)
   - Meeting cadence and norms
   - Communication channels and their purposes
   - Performance evaluation for researchers (non-standard: no KPIs for papers)
   - Onboarding new researchers (what they should know in Week 1)
   - Code of conduct and research integrity standards
   - Authorship and credit allocation policy
   - IP assignment and open source release policy

DOCUMENT 3 — TECHNICAL ROADMAP
   A systems and architecture plan (5–7 pages) covering:
   - Current infrastructure state
   - 6-month infrastructure goals
   - 2-year infrastructure goals
   - Compute acquisition plan
   - Software stack standardization
   - Security and reliability targets
   - Data infrastructure plan

DOCUMENT 4 — FINANCIAL MODEL
   A 3-year financial model (5–7 pages) covering:
   - Month-by-month burn rate projection (Year 1)
   - Quarter-by-quarter projection (Years 2–3)
   - Revenue scenarios: conservative, base, optimistic
   - Funding milestones and triggers
   - Cash runway at each scenario
   - Break-even analysis
   - Sensitivity analysis: what happens if compute costs 2× more?

DOCUMENT 5 — HIRING ROADMAP
   A talent plan (5–7 pages) covering:
   - Headcount plan: Year 1 (1), Year 2 (2–3), Year 3 (5–7), Year 5 (15–20)
   - For each planned hire: role, timing trigger, budget, skills required
   - Sourcing strategy for each role
   - Compensation bands (India market)
   - Equity pool structure and allocation
   - Intern and collaborator pipeline

DOCUMENT 6 — INFRASTRUCTURE BLUEPRINT
   A technical architecture document (5–7 pages) covering:
   - Current system architecture (ASCII diagram)
   - Compute: owned hardware + cloud strategy
   - Storage: active storage, archive, backup
   - Networking: security, VPN, access controls
   - Software: version-pinned stack
   - Monitoring and alerting setup
   - Disaster recovery plan
   - Cost optimization strategy

DOCUMENT 7 — RESEARCH AGENDA
   A research direction document (5–7 pages) covering:
   - Primary research focus (1 area, deeply)
   - Secondary research areas (adjacent, 1–2)
   - Why these areas, why now
   - Specific open problems being pursued
   - Expected output: papers, models, benchmarks, datasets
   - Collaboration targets: which labs or researchers to engage
   - 12-month research milestones
   - How the agenda will evolve in Years 2–5

DOCUMENT 8 — RISK ANALYSIS
   A comprehensive risk register (5–7 pages) covering:
   For each risk: likelihood (1–5), impact (1–5), mitigation strategy, owner
   
   Risk categories:
   - Financial (compute overrun, funding failure, revenue miss)
   - Research (research dead-end, paradigm shift, replication failure)
   - Team (key person departure, hiring failure, culture breakdown)
   - Technical (infrastructure failure, security breach, data loss)
   - Strategic (competitor supersedes work, timing error, wrong archetype choice)
   - Regulatory (AI regulation, data compliance, IP conflict)

DOCUMENT 9 — FUNDING STRATEGY
   A 5-year funding plan (5–7 pages) covering:
   - Year 0–1: bootstrapping + grants (non-dilutive)
   - Year 1–2: angel/seed if needed, key grant applications
   - Year 2–3: Series A triggers and preparation
   - Alternative path: grant-funded independent lab forever
   - Investor target list with rationale
   - Grant calendar: which grants to apply for, in what order
   - Revenue targets as alternative to fundraising

DOCUMENT 10 — DAILY OPERATING MANUAL
   The day-to-day protocol document (5–7 pages) covering:
   - Daily schedule template
   - Experiment review protocol
   - Paper writing workflow
   - Communication protocols (when to use email vs. Slack vs. GitHub Issues)
   - Meeting types and their cadence (daily standup, weekly review, monthly review)
   - Decision-making process for common decisions
   - Emergency protocols (compute failure, security breach, key person out)
   - Quarterly review process

Format: Each document as a standalone section with header, purpose, and full content.
Use tables, ASCII diagrams, and checklists throughout.
Total depth: ~30–40 pages across all 10 documents.
```

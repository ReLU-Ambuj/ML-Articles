# Section 6 — Organizational Structure

**Volume:** II — Research, Infrastructure & Organization  
**Status:** 🔲 Not Started  
**Estimated Depth:** 15–20 pages

---

## Objectives

1. Design a functional organizational chart that works at 1, 10, 50, and 200 people
2. Understand governance models that preserve research culture under commercial pressure
3. Know how frontier labs are actually organized internally (not just the org chart)
4. Build a documentation and communication system that scales

---

## Prompt for Deep Content Generation

```
You are a research lab director, organizational designer, and management theorist.

Write a graduate-level analysis of organizational structure for AI research labs 
as Section 6 of the manual "Building an AI Research Lab From Scratch."

PART A — ORGANIZATIONAL ARCHETYPES
Compare three organizational models and analyze which works best at which stage:

   Model 1: Flat Research Collective (academic-style)
   Model 2: Research Group + Engineering Team (FAIR-style)
   Model 3: Research Division + Product Division (OpenAI-style)

For each:
   - How decisions get made
   - How projects get started and killed
   - How credit and authorship get assigned
   - How conflict gets resolved
   - At what team size it breaks down and why

PART B — THE ACTUAL HIERARCHY
Design an org chart for each of these stages:

   Stage 1: Solo founder (1 person)
   Stage 2: First 3 people
   Stage 3: 10 people
   Stage 4: 25 people
   Stage 5: 100 people

For each stage, draw the reporting structure as an ASCII org chart.
Explain every reporting relationship and why it exists.

PART C — KEY POSITIONS IN DEPTH
For each of the following positions, explain:
   - The actual day-to-day responsibilities (not a job description)
   - What decisions they own vs. consult on vs. are informed about
   - What makes someone exceptional in this role
   - The failure modes of this role
   
   Positions: CEO/Founder, Chief Scientist, Research Director, 
   Research Scientist, Research Engineer, Head of Infrastructure, 
   Head of Data, Head of Safety/Alignment, Head of Product, 
   Head of Business Development.

PART D — GOVERNANCE AND DECISION MAKING
   - How does OpenAI's board structure work (and why it failed in Nov 2023)?
   - How does Anthropic's Long-Term Benefit Trust work?
   - What governance structure preserves research integrity while allowing commercial operation?
   - Safety board vs. scientific advisory board: when do you need each?
   - Decision-making frameworks: RACI, DRI, consent-based governance

PART E — COMMUNICATION SYSTEMS
   - How do frontier labs manage internal knowledge sharing?
   - Paper reading groups: how to run them effectively
   - Weekly research presentations: structure and cadence
   - Async communication: Slack vs. Discord vs. Notion for research teams
   - Lab notebook / experiment journal culture
   - Internal blog / research memo culture (Google's "20% time" memos, 
     OpenAI's internal model cards)

PART F — SCALING WITHOUT LOSING CULTURE
   - The "soul of the lab" problem: what happens when you hit 50 people?
   - How to maintain research quality as the team grows
   - The publication-vs-product tension and how to manage it structurally
   - How DeepMind maintained academic culture inside Google

Format: structured markdown with ASCII org charts, 
decision matrix tables, communication system recommendations.
Depth: ~15–20 pages.
```

## Funding Landscape Map

```
Funding Sources
├── Non-dilutive
│   ├── Research Grants (NSF, DARPA, NIH, Wellcome Trust, DST, SERB)
│   ├── Government AI Programs (MEITY, NITI Aayog, EU Horizon)
│   ├── Philanthropy (Schmidt Futures, Open Philanthropy, Skoll Foundation)
│   ├── Open Source Sponsorship (GitHub Sponsors, NLnet, SustainOSS)
│   ├── Corporate Grants (Google TPU Research Cloud, AWS Research Credits)
│   └── Revenue (bootstrapping from services/API)
├── Dilutive
│   ├── Angel Investment
│   ├── Pre-seed / Seed VC
│   ├── Series A / B VC
│   ├── Strategic Investment (Big Tech)
│   └── Sovereign Wealth Funds
└── Mixed
    ├── University Partnership (compute + talent, no equity)
    └── Government Contracts (revenue, some IP restrictions)
```

## Prompt for Deep Content Generation

```
You are a venture capitalist, research lab founder, and grant writing expert 
writing a funding guide for AI research lab founders.

Write Section 15 of "Building an AI Research Lab From Scratch" — 
a complete guide to funding an AI research lab.

PART A — NON-DILUTIVE FUNDING IN DEPTH
For each of the following, provide:
   - How to access it (application process, eligibility, timeline)
   - Realistic funding amount range
   - What the funder wants (research agenda fit, reporting requirements)
   - How it affects research independence
   - Success rate and competition level
   - Strategic use: how to use this funding to unlock the next funding stage

Sources:
   1. NSF (US): AI Institute grants, CAREER, SBIR/STTR
   2. DARPA: how DARPA funding works, which programs are active
   3. NIH: AI in biomedicine programs
   4. DST / SERB (India): NPDF, CRG, IMPRINT programs
   5. MEITY / NITI Aayog (India): AI mission funding, IndiaAI program
   6. EU Horizon Europe: ERC grants, MSCA fellowships
   7. Google: Google Research Scholar Program, TPU Research Cloud
   8. NVIDIA: Academic GPU Program, DLI credits
   9. AWS / Azure / GCP: startup credits programs
   10. Open Philanthropy: AI safety and fundamental research grants
   11. Schmidt Futures: Eric and Wendy Schmidt AI grants
   12. GitHub Sponsors / Open Collective: community funding

PART B — DILUTIVE FUNDING IN DEPTH
   - Angel investment: what angels look for in an AI research lab,
     how to find them, typical check sizes ($25K–$250K), terms
   - Pre-seed: what a pre-seed round looks like for a research lab,
     typical valuation, terms, what you give up
   - Seed: timeline, typical amount ($1–5M), what milestones unlock it
   - Series A: when it makes sense, typical metrics required, lead investors
   - Strategic investment: why Amazon/Google invest in Anthropic, 
     what the trade-offs are (IP, exclusivity, compute commitments)
   - Sovereign wealth funds: Middle Eastern and Asian SWF investment in AI

PART C — THE FUNDING DECISION FRAMEWORK
   - When to raise vs. when NOT to raise
   - The "funding trap": how raising too early kills research independence
   - The research lab vs. startup distinction in investor eyes
   - How to structure a raise that preserves research mission
   - Convertible notes vs. SAFE vs. priced rounds for early research labs
   - The PBC (Public Benefit Corporation) or nonprofit hybrid model
   - Anthropic's Long-Term Benefit Trust: how it works, pros and cons

PART D — GRANT WRITING THAT WINS
   - The anatomy of a winning NSF / DST research proposal
   - How to write a problem statement that reviewers rank highly
   - Budget justification: how to cost-justify ML compute for grants
   - The "broader impact" section: what it means and how to write it
   - Common grant writing mistakes for ML researchers
   - Grant writing tools: how AI can help (within ethical limits)

PART E — THE BOOTSTRAPPING PATH
   - Revenue-first approach: consulting, custom model training, API access
   - The "research consultancy to lab" playbook
   - How DeepMind (early) and Mila fund themselves
   - What bootstrapping looks like in India specifically
   - The minimum viable product that funds research

PART F — INDIA-SPECIFIC FUNDING LANDSCAPE
   - IndiaAI Mission: budget, programs, eligibility
   - iDEX (Innovations for Defence Excellence): AI programs
   - Atal Innovation Mission: incubators and grants
   - NASSCOM AI programs and partnerships
   - Indian VC landscape for deep tech: Blume, Kalaari, Stellaris, Accel India
   - International foundations with India operations

PART G — PITCH STRATEGY
   - How to pitch a research lab (not a startup) to VCs
   - The research lab pitch deck: what goes in, what doesn't
   - How to talk about research timelines to non-technical investors
   - Metrics that resonate: papers at tier-1 venues, model benchmark rankings, 
     open source traction, researcher quality
   - The "why now" for a research lab: timing your pitch

Format: structured markdown, funding source comparison table,
grant writing template, pitch deck outline.
Depth target: ~20–25 pages.
```
